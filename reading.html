Reading

A World Of My Own

Reading feels like stepping into a world that exists beyond reality, a space created entirely by imagination. It allows me to disconnect from the real world and immerse myself in stories filled with fantasy, thrill, adventure and experiences that could never happen in everyday life. Every book becomes a quiet escape, offering comfort and curiosity at the same time.

Falling in Love With Fiction

Since my teenage years, I have been a devoted fan of the Harry Potter novels. The magic, imagination and emotional depth of the series made reading an unforgettable experience. The world-building was so captivating that it didn’t just tell a story, it made me live it, shaping my love for fictional worlds.

Discovering The Classics

As I grew older, my reading journey expanded to classic literature. Works by Charles Dickens, especially Great Expectations and Oliver Twist, fascinated me with their storytelling, characters and social depth. These novels offered a different kind of richness, thought-provoking yet deeply engaging.

Light Reads

During periods of stress, especially while preparing for competitive exams, light-hearted books became my refuge. Reading something fun and easy helped take my mind off pressure and deadlines. The Diary of a Wimpy Kid series is one such example, simple, humorous and comforting during overwhelming days.

Reflection

I also enjoy philosophical and deeper novels that leave lasting impressions. The Alchemist is one such book that stood out, offering reflections on dreams, purpose and self-discovery. These stories don’t just entertain, they quietly stay with you long after you have finished reading.

A Habit That Still Lives On

Although my reading habit has declined over the years due to busy schedules and fewer opportunities to explore new stories, it remains a pastime close to my heart. Even if I don’t read as much as I once did, the love for books, stories and imagined worlds has never truly faded, it is still a comfort I return to whenever I can.
